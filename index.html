About Me

Hi, I'm Sreevarshini. I build the infrastructure that data and AI systems run on.

I started out moving terabytes of retail data across the Middle East for Loyalytics AI: untangling years of nested XML data, keeping medallion pipelines running on Delta Live Tables, making sure nothing silently broke when a schema changed upstream. That's where I learned that most "AI problems" are actually data problems wearing a costume. A model can only be as good as the pipeline feeding it.

From there I moved into cloud infrastructure at DriverAI, provisioning AWS and Azure environments with Terraform and wiring up the monitoring to know when something's about to go wrong before it does. I like that side of the work that makes everything else possible.

Now I'm finishing my master's in Data Science at Maryland, which has pulled me back toward the algorithmic side: transformers, time-series models, the kind of problems where the data is messy and irregular and the stakes are real.

What ties it together is a stubborn interest in reliability. I'd rather build something boring and correct than clever and fragile. Systems that catch their own mistakes, pipelines that tell you when they're lying to you, models that know when to say "I'm not sure" — that's the stuff I actually enjoy building.

Outside of work, I'm the person people at the Tinkerspace lab come to when the Arduino Cloud times out for no reason. I am also exploring ML on physical devices on the side in the lab.
